## About Me

Graduate student at **Carnegie Mellon University** (Kigali), pursuing an MS in Electrical and Computer Engineering (2024–2026).

My research focuses on **fairness-aware biometric systems**, specifically face presentation attack detection and mitigating skin tone bias in anti-spoofing models. I am a co-author of a peer-reviewed paper published in the *Journal of Cybersecurity and Privacy* (MDPI, 2026).

I build and experiment with **deep learning, NLP, large language models (LLMs), and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)** pipelines. Currently working on a multi-turn RAG system for the SemEval competition that combines BM25 sparse retrieval with FAISS dense retrieval to reduce hallucinations in LLM responses.

I am also passionate about **data science and analytics**, transforming raw data into actionable insights through rigorous statistical analysis, predictive modelling, and end-to-end ML pipelines. Beyond research, I have a solid background in **full-stack software engineering**, having built web and mobile applications across multiple internships.

Currently serving as a **Graduate Teaching Assistant** for *Introduction to Deep Learning (11-785)* at CMU, helping students navigate neural network architectures, training pipelines, and modern optimisation techniques.

I care deeply about building AI that is **responsible, equitable, and impactful**, especially for underrepresented communities. Outside of tech, I unwind through drawing and painting.

📬 **ftuyisen@andrew.cmu.edu**
